Peaches and Cream Ice Cream

Added July 20, 2019

Here is a delicious and simple ice cream you can make this summer that is vegan and tastes delicious. It uses fresh fruit for flavor instead of artificial flavorings.

Prep time: 15 mins

Total time: 3 hours

Servings: 6 scoops

My Vegan Ice cream base

  • 1 14 oz can of full fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup of almond millk
  • 3 Tbsp coconut oil
  • 3/4 Tbsp. guar gum
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp lemon zest+ 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp almond extract paste
  • 1/2 cup coconut nectar
  • 2 peaches, peeled, chopped, pits removed, keep 1/2 a cup chopped pieces for topping

Process

  1. Mix all ice cream base ingredients together in a blender until smooth. Chill base in a refrigerator covered for 2-3 hours.
  2. Pour ice cream base into an ice cream maker and follow manufacturer's instructions. Once ice cream has thickened up, remove and scoop out into a container.
  3. Top ice cream with crushed hazelnuts and fresh peach slices and enjoy!

Kitchen Tips

Do you have a hard time digesting beans? Did you know if you soak dry beans overnight before cooking this can do a number of things

  • This will make the beans more digestible, as the soaking helps remove the indigestable sugars that cause flatulence.
  • This will also shorten the cooking time

There are 2 simple ways to get this done!

REGULAR SOAK: Put beans into a large bowl and cover with 2 to 3 inches of cool, clean water. Set aside at room temperature for 8 hours or overnight; drain well. (If it's really warm in your kitchen, soak the beans in the refrigerator instead to avoid fermentation.)

If you forget the overnight soak and need them in a pinch! QUICK SOAK: Put beans into a large pot and cover with 2 to 3 inches of cool, clean water. Bring to a boil then boil briskly for 2 to 3 minutes. Cover and set aside off of the heat for 1 hour; drain well.

Cooking Process: Once beans have been soaked.... Cook: Put beans into a large pot and cover with 2 inches of water or stock. (Don't add salt at this point since that slows the beans' softening.) Slowly bring to a boil, skimming off any foam on the surface. Reduce heat, cover and simmer, stirring occasionally and adding more liquid if necessary, until beans are tender when mashed or pierced with a fork. Cooking times vary with the variety, age and size of beans; generally you're looking at about 1 to 2 hours.
